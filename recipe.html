Bitterleaf Soup (Ofe Onugbu)

Bitterleaf soup – also known as Ofe Onugbu – is a beloved Nigerian delicacy enjoyed by the Igbo people and beyond. This hearty dish is flavorful, versatile, and can be made in many different ways. It also keeps well when refrigerated, making it perfect for batch cooking.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 30 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour 20 minutes

Servings: 12 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Dish Photos

Ingredients

  • 2 kg cow head (meat)
  • Bitterleaf (washed to desired bitterness)
  • 1/2 cup ground crayfish
  • 3 cubes Maggi or Knorr seasoning
  • Ogiri (optional, fermented castor seed product)
  • 2 medium-sized dry fish
  • 1 large stockfish head
  • 25 cl palm oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pounded cocoyam (thickener)
  • 1/2 cup ofor (alternative thickener)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the leaves: Boil the bitterleaf in plain water for 10–15 minutes to soften and reduce bitterness. Avoid potash if possible, but if used, rinse thoroughly afterward.
  2. Cook the meat: Parboil the cow head with 2 seasoning cubes, salt, and chopped onions for 10 minutes. Then add water and cook until tender.
  3. Add fish: Wash dry fish and stockfish with hot water, then add to the pot. Cook until tender.
  4. Flavor the broth: Add palm oil, ground crayfish, pepper, remaining seasoning, salt, and ogiri. Stir well and simmer.
  5. Thicken the soup: Add pounded cocoyam (or egusi if desired) and cook until dissolved. Add ofor if more thickness is needed.
  6. Final touch: Add the bitterleaf, stir, adjust seasoning, and cook for 2–3 more minutes.

Tips

Serve with Eba, Pounded Yam, Fufu, Wheat, or Semo for an authentic Nigerian experience. For best results, avoid overcooking the bitterleaf to preserve its aroma and nutrients.
